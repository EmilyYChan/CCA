What we do

A fast-paced, week-long introduction to coding in Python, designed to educate and inspire. Absolutely no coding experience required—our course is meant to be accessible to anyone and everyone. For details, check our FAQ and Curriculum. Held in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

How we do it

Our unique, thoughtfully designed program combines constant support in the classroom with challenging problem sets that familiarize students with a wide variety of CS concepts. Students are given the chance to tackle problems independently, while being carefully guided through the problem solving process.

We believe anyone can code

We welcome students of all abilities, ages, races, religions, ethnicities, & gender identities. CCA is an inclusive and supportive environment where students of all backgrounds can learn together. Black, Hispanic, and female students are especially encouraged to apply.

FAQ

Everything you need to know about CCA.

Who teaches the program?

All of our instructors are Harvard Computer Science majors, and our primary instructor is a Harvard CS major with several years of teaching and tutoring experience. All instructors undergo minors protection certification as well as several hours of teacher training to teach effectively and maintain a comfortable classroom dynamic.

What is the size of the class?

Each session will have approximately 15-20 students, though more or less is possible depending on interest in a given session. However, the number of instructors will adjust according to the size of the class to ensure plenty of individual support for students.

Where will the program be held?

The program will be held on the Harvard campus. Due to booking restrictions, the exact location is to be determined, and will be confirmed within a week of the start of the program.

Will lunch be provided?

Due to the difficulty of organizing orders that accommodate the variety of allergies and dietary restrictions/preferences, lunch will not be provided. Please bring a packed lunch or money to order food. The program will be within walking distance of either Harvard Square or Mass Ave.

What is the pace of the program?

Intensive. We pack a lot of computer science into one week, and we expect students to stay focused and dedicated to doing their best, regardless of what level they might be at coming into the course.

What topics will be covered?

The curriculum is based on Harvard’s introductory CS class, CS50. For details, check the curriculum. Please note, NO EXPERIENCE is needed for this program. It can serve as a review for those who are familiar with another language and wish to learn Python, but this program teaches computer science concepts using Python—the focus is not on teaching Python itself.

Is this program right for me?

We would like to underscore that everyone can do computer science, even if you don’t like math or aren’t interested in computers. Coding is a useful, practical, and impressive skill, but more importantly, it teaches a way of thinking and working through problems that is valuable for any future endeavors, whatever field they may be in. The only background required is a basic understanding of grade school level math. The program will be useful to anyone without a background in computer science who is excited to learn, but it is targeted towards middle and high school students.

Why Python?

As the most popular programming language in the world, Python is the backbone behind Google, Youtube, Spotify, Instagram, Dropbox, Quora, reddit, and even games like Civilization IV and the Sims 4. Despite being common in the industry, Python is generally not taught in schools—AP Computer Science uses Java, while more basic intro courses use Scratch. Python is a higher level programming language (as opposed to lower level languages such as C), meaning that it resembles the English language much more than machine code. This makes it accessible to new programmers, and relatively easy to demonstrate computer science concepts with.

Is financial assistance available?

Unfortunately, due to the small size of the academy, we cannot offer direct financial assistance; however, depending on the size of the classroom available, and the number of other students who enroll, we may allow students to audit the class (they would be able to observe the lectures and attempt problems on their own, but not receive individual help from teaching fellows). If our program poses a financial burden, and this option is something you wish to pursue, please contact us at cambridgecodingacademy.ma@gmail.com.

For further questions, please contact us at cambridgecodingacademy.ma@gmail.com.
